Kino Archives - Memories of a bygone era.

The Kino Archive is dedicated to preserving the band’s recorded history, rare footage, rehearsal materials, and significant events that shaped their legacy. This section gathers concerts, interviews and restored media from across Kino’s career, allowing fans to experience how the band evolved and how their music resonated with audiences throughout the Soviet Union.

Over the course of eight years of Victor Tsoi's direciton, Kino released over ninety songs that spans over seven albums.

TIMELINE OF KINO:

1981 - Formation

Kino was formed in Leningrad by Viktor Tsoi, Aleksei Rybin, and Maksim Pashkov.

1982 - First Album & Leningrad Rock Club

Their first album 45 is released.

Kino begins performing at the Leningrad Rock Club.

1983 - Early Performances & New Lineup

Kino performs in Leningrad and Moscow.

Yuri Kasparyan joins as lead guitarist.

1984 - Nachalnik Kamchatki & Growing Popularity

Kino released their third full-length album, Nachalnik Kamchatki(Chief of Kamchatka).

They performed at the II Leningrad Rock Club Festival, where audience reaction significantly boosted their rising popularity within the Soviet rock movement.

1985 - Eto ne lyubov...

Kino released their fourth album, Eto ne lyubov... (This is not love...).

1986 - Noch, Festival Success & Western Recognition

Kino released their fourth album, Noch (Night).

During the IV Rock Club Festival, the band won the Grand Prize for their performance of “Dalshe deystvovat budem my.”

In July, Kino performed at the Moscow Palace of Culture of Engineering Workers, reaching a broader audience.

Kino appeared on the influential compilation Red Wave along with two other bands, making them one of the first rock acts from the USSR to be officially distributed in the West.

1987 - Gruppa Krovi Recording Begins & The Last Hero

Kino began working on their upcoming landmark album of Gruppa Krovi(Blood Type).

Kino releases Posledniy Geroy (The Last Hero), a concert album that is initally released in France and then domestically, later became one of their most widely distributed live records outside the USSR.

1988 - Gruppa Krovi Released & Rising Fame

Kino released Gruppa Krovi (Blood Type), one of the most iconic albums in Russian rock history. Its themes of personal struggle, anti-war sentiment, and generational frustration solidified Kino’s cultural status.

The band performed throughout the Eastern Bloc and received invitations from Western countries, including France and Denmark.

Viktor Tsoi starred in the feature film The Needle, which became a cult classic. The soundtrack prominently featured Kino songs, including Gruppa Krovi and Zvezda po imeni Solntse. The film dramatically expanded Tsoi’s visibility across the USSR.

1989 - Zvezda po imeni Solntse

Kino released Zvezda po imeni Solntse (A Star Called the Sun), another defining album known for its darker, more atmospheric tone.

The band continued touring and reached the peak of their popularity, drawing enormous crowds across the Soviet Union.

1990 - Tsoi’s Passing & The Black Album

On August 15, Viktor Tsoi died in a car accident in Latvia due to sleep exhaustion.

The surviving members of Kino completed the unfinished studio recordings, resulting in the album Chyorny Albom (The Black Album), released later that year. Though officially untitled, it stands as Kino’s final record and a tribute to Tsoi.

2012 - Anniversary Recording: “Ataman”

On the 50th anniversary of Viktor Tsoi’s birth, surviving members reunited to complete and release “Ataman”, a previously unfinished Kino track.

2019-2021 - Kino Reunion Concerts

In 2019, Kino announced a modern reunion, featuring:.

  • Yuri Kasparyan
  • Alexander Titov
  • Igor Tikhomirov

The tour was initially scheduled for 2020 but postponed due to the COVID-19 pandemic, eventually launching in 2021 with major concerts across Russia and Europe.

2022–2025 - Modern Releases & Restorations

New Kino projects included:

  • Remastered album editions
  • Restored concert footage
  • Expanded reunion tours
  • New live recordings featuring Kasparyan and the modern lineup

The Last Concert

Kino’s last concert took place on June 24 1990, Moscow's Luzhniki Stadium. Approximately 70,000 people visited the stadium even though it was rainy that day. It was the last concert on band's nationwide tour but no one knew that it would be their last.

On August 15 1990, Victor Tsoi passed away in a car crash that shocked the band and their fans to the point where some of the Kino fans took their own lives in response. This video is part of that concert where the band plays Gruppa Krovi, their most iconic song.

War!

Voyna!(War!) is a song released in 1986 as part of the band's album "Nachalnik Kamchatki" (The Chief of Kamchatka).

The song's lyrics are a critique of war and violence, focusing on the absurdity and destructiveness of conflict. The chorus, which repeats "Voyna!" ("War!"), highlights the song's main theme of the chaotic and relentless nature of war, while the verses reflect on how it affects individuals and society.The lyric "Between Earth and Heaven, there is war!" symbolizes that war always existed as part of the human nature and carries on as time passes by.

Though it's not an explicitly reflected as an anti-Soviet song, "Voyna" symbolized a call for peace and a condemnation of conflict, especially the senselessness of war that affects ordinary people. Tsoi's lyrics often had a poetic, symbolic quality, and "Voyna" is no exception, using imagery and emotional appeals to make a powerful statement about the horrors of war while accepting that it's part of human nature.

Reunion Concert - Spokoynaya Noch

Spokoynaya Noch" (which means "Good Night" in Russian) is a song by Kino, one of the most influential rock bands from Russia.

The song has a very poetic and somewhat haunting message. It talks about a sense of finality, the end of an era, and the inevitability of change. The lyrics reflect a bittersweet acceptance of the moment, mixed with the desire for peace.

Spokoynaya Noch was played in CSKA Arena, Moscow as part of a reunion concert of Kino where Yuri Kasparyan, Igor Tikhomirov, Georgy Guryanov, Aleksei Rybin and Oleg Valinsky performed together.
